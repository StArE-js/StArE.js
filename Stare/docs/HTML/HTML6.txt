<p><b>Taza</b> (also <i>Tazi; Tazhe; Tah-ze; Tahzi; Tahzay; Tazhay</i>) (c. 1843 – 26 September 1876) was the son of <a href="/wiki/Cochise" title="Cochise">Cochise</a>, leader of the Chihuicahui local group of the Chokonen and principal chief of the Chokonen band of the <a href="/wiki/Chiricahua_Apache" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiricahua Apache">Chiricahua Apache</a> and his principal wife <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dos-teh-seh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dos-teh-seh (page does not exist)">Dos-teh-seh</a> (“Something-at-the-campfire-already-cooked”), the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Mangus_Coloradas" class="mw-redirect" title="Mangus Coloradas">Mangus Coloradas</a>, leader of the Copper Mines and last leader of the Mimbreños local groups of the Chihenne band and principal chief of the Chihenne band of the Chiricahua Apache.<sup id="cite_ref-Thrapp1991_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thrapp1991-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chief_Taza">Chief Taza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza_(Chiricahua_leader)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chief Taza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taza was the older full brother of <a href="/wiki/Naiche" title="Naiche">Naiche</a> (Natchez). Taza succeeded his father Cochise as chief of the Chiricahuas when the latter died in 1874, two years after the Chiricahua Reservation was established by General Howard.<sup id="cite_ref-Sweeney2012_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sweeney2012-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1876 the tribe was removed from the Chiricahua reservation to San Carlos, and in September of the same year Taza was one of a delegation of Apaches taken to Washington D.C. for a visit. He fell ill in Washington and died there of pneumonia on September 26, 1876, after only about two years as chief.<sup id="cite_ref-Cozzens2016_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cozzens2016-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is buried in <a href="/wiki/Congressional_Cemetery" title="Congressional Cemetery">Congressional Cemetery</a> Washington D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-Miles2014_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Miles2014-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (December 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Taza’s mother Dos-teh-seh, his brother Naiche, and his half-sisters Dash-den-zhoos and Naithlotonz (Naiche-dos), lived through the prisoner-of-war period and went to Mescalero.<sup id="cite_ref-Robinson2003_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson2003-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is unknown if a portrait of Taza was ever made; an alleged "photograph" of Taza was actually made of Native American named George Noche in 1886<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
