<p><b>Taza</b> (<a href="/wiki/Berber_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Berber language">Berber</a>: ⵜⴰⵣⴰ, Taza, in <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>: تازة) is a city in northern <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, which occupies the corridor between the <a href="/wiki/Rif" title="Rif">Rif</a> mountains and Middle Atlas mountains, about 120&#160;km east of <a href="/wiki/Fez,_Morocco" title="Fez, Morocco">Fez</a> and 210&#160;km west of <a href="/wiki/Oujda" title="Oujda">Oujda</a>. It recorded a population of 148,456 in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Moroccan_census" title="2014 Moroccan census">2014 Moroccan census</a><sup id="cite_ref-census2014_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census2014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Taza_Province" title="Taza Province">Taza Province</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically Taza was known first as ' <i>Ribāt Taza رباط تازة '</i> , a military camp belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Fatimid" class="mw-redirect" title="Fatimid">Fatimid</a> state , was founded by the local governor <i>Mussa ibn aby Alaâfiya موسى بن أبي العافية </i> who was the leader of <a href="/wiki/Miknasa" title="Miknasa">Miknassa</a> tribe <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> too. Up to at least the early 20th century, Taza was considerable trading centre on the route between Fez and the Algerian frontier.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taza is located in north-central Morocco, in the south of the <a href="/wiki/Rif" title="Rif">Rif</a> region right outside the mountain range on a narrow plain. The city is composed of two formerly separate towns built on separate terraces overlooking a mountain valley. The old-Taza town is at an elevation of 1,919 feet (585 m) above sea level and is surrounded by fortifications; the newer town, established by the French in 1920, is located in a fertile plain at an elevation of 1,460 feet (445 m). <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">Fossil</a> remains indicate that caves in the area were inhabited as early as the <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Paleolithic Period">Paleolithic Period</a>. One of the most important caves in Morocco, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhar_Chara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rhar Chara (page does not exist)">Rhar Chara</a>, is close to Taza. This cave is over 7.6 kilometres long.<sup id="cite_ref-gunn_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gunn-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The city is located in a mountain pass known as the "Taza Gap", where the Rif mountains and the Middle-Atlas range come together. Through this pass successive waves of invaders moved westward onto the Atlantic coastal plains of northwestern Africa. Taza was first settled by <a href="/wiki/Miknasa" title="Miknasa">Miknasa</a> tribesmen, who gave it its name: <i>Miknasa Taza</i>, similar to <i>Miknasa al-Zeitoun</i> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Meknes" title="Meknes">Meknes</a>, another Miknasa settlement). The <a href="/wiki/Almoravid_dynasty" title="Almoravid dynasty">Almoravid</a> empire took over Taza in 1074. They were replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Almohad_dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Almohad dynasty">Almohad</a> empire in 1132. In 1248 the city was captured by the <a href="/wiki/Marinid_dynasty" title="Marinid dynasty">Marinids</a>. Although Taza barred the route of Turks from <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a> seeking conquest in what is now <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> it fell to the French in 1914. The old town has barbican monuments, mosques, and a 14th-century <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mderasa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mderasa (page does not exist)">mderasa</a> (Coranic school). Population in 1982 stood at 77,216. Population now estimated about 300,000.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen-Geiger_climate_classification_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Köppen-Geiger climate classification system">Köppen-Geiger climate classification system</a> classifies its climate as <a href="/wiki/Hot-summer_Mediterranean_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot-summer Mediterranean climate">hot-summer Mediterranean</a> (Csa).<sup id="cite_ref-Climate-Data.org_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Climate-Data.org-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Located along the Atlas Mountains, Taza has a seasonal climate, shifting from cool in winter to hot days in the summer months. Rainfall can reach up to 900&#160;mm per year.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The old town's main thoroughfare is enlivened by the Grain Market and the <a href="/wiki/Souk" class="mw-redirect" title="Souk">Souks</a> where wickerwork, tapestries, jewelry, ... The road terminates at a square doubling as a parade ground which sports the Al-Andalous Mosque. The Mosque's minaret, constructed in the 14th century, is wider at the top than at its base.
Bab el-Qebbour Street crosses the Kissaria (covered marketplace), then leads on to the Market Mosque where it meets up with Bab Jamaa gate, the main point of entry of Taza. Somewhat further south, across from Bab el-Rih, the Wind Gate, a bastion dating from the 16th century closes the ring around the <a href="/wiki/Kasbah" title="Kasbah">kasbah</a>. Taza's city walls, raised in the 12th century and frequently enlarged on later occasions were equipped with a Borj or fortified tower 26 metres (85&#160;ft) wide at the base by the  <a href="/wiki/Saadi_dynasty" title="Saadi dynasty">Saadi</a> <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_I_al-Mansur_Saadi" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahmad I al-Mansur Saadi">Ahmed el-Mansour</a> in the 16th century. The gate with  iron grate and the <a href="/wiki/Casemate" title="Casemate">casemates</a> with terraced roofs are clearly influenced by European military architecture of the time.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Topology">Topology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Topology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The topology of the area has imposed a pattern of urban spread. Viewed from above, the city takes the form of a "T". It has its root in Taza high and stretches north to reach the bed of the Oued Larbaâ. Since the urban stretches east and west along the N6 between Fez and Oujda.
</p><p>At the dawn of independence, the city consisted of the Medina High Taza, the European district occupying almost hill called "No Adrar Illouz. People usually pronounce Draâ louz. This area became the center of town, and finally the station area a few kilometers below.
</p><p>During the following years (60s, 70s) districts have emerged, midway between downtown and the train station (Bit Goulem, Ourida, Bin-Jradi).
</p><p>During the 80s, other districts make their appearance, especially north of the city (and Massira Al-Quds), extended and developed areas today. The goal of this extension was the <a href="/wiki/Slum_clearance" title="Slum clearance">slum clearance</a>. It was a success, since 1986 the city was declared a city without slums. The craze is such that sections like (Massira II) are villa style.
</p><p>The 90s marked the beginning of the urbanization of the axis center - Taza above. These are buildings with 6 or 7 floors occupying a strategic content between public facilities (municipality, space, civil protection, hospital ibn baja, high school and college ...) and on the other hand, it is down the high rock a hundred yards. This area is also adjacent to the uptown-Qessou meddah, Friouato and Hay Shuhada (developed throughout the last three decades).
</p><p>More recently this area continues to morph and promises a beautiful view from the heights of the city. The planning now also spread on the road to Fez for several kilometers to reach the intermittently R508 (about Tainast).
</p><p>The development plan provides a direct link between Taza West (at the "white bridge") and Taza high.
</p>
