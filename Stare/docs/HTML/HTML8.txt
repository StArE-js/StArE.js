<p>The <b>Taza National Park</b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>:الحظيرة الوطنية تازة) is one of the smaller <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Algeria" class="mw-redirect" title="List of national parks of Algeria">national parks of Algeria</a>. It is located in <a href="/wiki/Jijel_Province" title="Jijel Province">Jijel Province</a> in the region of the <a href="/wiki/Tell_Atlas" title="Tell Atlas">Tell Atlas</a>, and is named after the nearby city of Taza. Its total area is 3,807 hectares (9,410 acres) and it includes parts of the forested area of the <a href="/wiki/Guerrouch" title="Guerrouch">Guerrouch massif</a>. The lower parts of the park seldom experience frost and are relatively warm and dry, while the peaks may have a covering of snow in winter. The annual precipitation in the park ranges from 1,000 to 1,400 millimetres (39 to 55&#160;in) and the annual mean temperature is around 18&#160;°C (64&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-Harrap2010_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrap2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_park">The park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taza National Park was established in 1923 by the French colonial administrators in Algeria. Its aim was to protect the coastline and towering forested heights and provide protection for the <a href="/wiki/Barbary_macaque" title="Barbary macaque">Barbary macaque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Haggett2001_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett2001-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The park adjoins the Mediterranean Sea, and includes cliffs that rise from sea level to over 1,100&#160;m (3,600&#160;ft). The largest forest in Algeria of <a href="/wiki/Quercus_suber" title="Quercus suber">cork oaks</a> (<i>Quercus suber</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Quercus_faginea" title="Quercus faginea">gall oaks</a> (<i>Quercus faginea</i>) is found here.<sup id="cite_ref-Ham_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ham-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The park is located 30 kilometres north-east of <a href="/wiki/Jijel" title="Jijel">Jijel</a>. The park includes the <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">caves</a> of Jijel, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Sand_beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Sand beach">sand beaches</a> and many <a href="/wiki/Cliff" title="Cliff">cliffs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grotto" title="Grotto">grottoes</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>-recognized <a href="/wiki/Biosphere_reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Biosphere reserve">biosphere reserve</a> with a varied <a href="/wiki/Flora" title="Flora">flora</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">fauna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biosphere_reserve">Biosphere reserve</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biosphere reserve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Taza Biosphere Reserve comprises the whole of Taza National Park. It aims to combine conservation of the environment with the sustainable use of natural resources,  allowing the local economy to flourish. Some of the habitats, plants and wildlife are important or unique in North Africa and internationally. About 5,600 people, mostly of <a href="/wiki/Berbers" title="Berbers">Berber</a> origin, live inside the reserve, largely sustaining themselves on small agricultural holdings, cultivating vegetables, fodder crops and tree fruits. Tourism is also important to the local economy. Management of the reserve includes eco-development projects, with beehives and fruit tree seed being distributed to villagers, and forest tracks and electricity supplies being improved for remote communities.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taza_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At lower altitudes, the Guerrouch forest is largely dominated by cork oaks, but higher up <a href="/wiki/Quercus_afares" title="Quercus afares">Algerian chestnut-leaved oak</a> (<i>Quercus afares</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Quercus_canariensis" title="Quercus canariensis">Algerian oak</a> (<i>Quercus canariensis</i>) are also present and there is a plentiful <a href="/wiki/Understorey" class="mw-redirect" title="Understorey">understorey</a> of <a href="/wiki/Prunus_avium" title="Prunus avium">bird cherry</a> (<i>Prunus avium</i>), <a href="/wiki/Salix_pedicellata" title="Salix pedicellata">Mediterranean willow</a> (<i>Salix pedicellata</i>), <a href="/wiki/Alnus_glutinosa" title="Alnus glutinosa">European alder</a> (<i>Alnus glutinosa</i>), <a href="/wiki/Acer_monspessulanum" title="Acer monspessulanum">Montpellier maple</a> (<i>Acer monspessulanum</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Fraxinus_angustifolia" title="Fraxinus angustifolia">narrow-leaved ash</a> (<i>Fraxinus angustifolia</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Harrap2010_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrap2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Barbary_ape" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbary ape">barbary ape</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Endangered" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered">endangered</a> <a href="/wiki/Primate" title="Primate">primate</a> with a narrowly restricted present distribution and is present in the park.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The endangered <a href="/wiki/Algerian_nuthatch" title="Algerian nuthatch">Algerian nuthatch</a> is also present, with an estimated population of 364 individuals in a 1989 survey.<sup id="cite_ref-Harrap2010_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrap2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The park is also known for its <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">birds of prey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ham_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ham-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Earlier, the <a href="/wiki/Barbary_lion" title="Barbary lion">Barbary lion</a>'s range had included this place.<sup id="cite_ref-Pease1913_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pease1913-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
